Who Am I?

I began my career as an ABAP Developer at IBM, where I worked for 5+ years across the full software development lifecycle. While gaining solid technical experience, I noticed key challenges in traditional software delivery such as long release cycles, limited automation, and slower integration processes.To address these, the teams I collaborated with began streamlining processes using DevOps practices, which gave me hands-on experience to CI/CD pipelines, automated releases, and containerized environments. This experience sparked my interest and motivated my transition into DevOps where I worked for about 2+ years.

During my Master’s in Information Systems at the University of Texas at Arlington, I was exposed to cloud computing, data science, Python, and project management. This exposure, combined with my experience at IBM, made me start thinking critically about how modern technologies could solve the challenges I had seen firsthand.

This motivated my transition into DevOps, where I began applying modern practices, building automated pipelines, scalable infrastructure, and cloud-based deployments through hands-on projects.
